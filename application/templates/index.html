FAQ

1

Q: What is the difference between an active shooter incidents and a mass shooting?

A: Active shooter incidents are unique because an aspect of the crime may affect the protocol in responding to the scene. Both law enforcement and citizens have the potential to impact the outcome of an active shooter incident. Therefore, all mass shootings are active shooter incidents; however, not all active shooter incidents are mass shootings because of the minimum four deaths to meet the mass shooting criteria.

2

Q: Does University of Oregon currently have an active shooter training program?

A: Yes they currently do, however as stated previously they are not mandated by the University. They live exclusively on the University’s Emergency Risk Services site. For students, in person training sessions need to be scheduled 3-6 weeks in advance with a group of fellow students. Based on our survey, 73% of 100+ faculty and staff have not attended or used these trainings.

3

Q: Are you guys focusing on this stuff as part of a conspiracy to take away Americans' gun rights?

A: No. Clearly the current rhetoric is not working as we are still experiencing an increase in active shooter incidents is undeniable. We are simply looking for a way to increase the safety of faculty, staff and students on campus. We wish not to engage in the politically-weighted debate over firearm rights.

4

Q: By signing this petition what exactly am I asking the University?

A: By signing this petition, you agree with the statement that the University of Oregon should do more to ensure students safety i.e. provide active shooter training. The University of Oregon has the opportunity to further leverage federal resources made available to institutions of higher education in order to increase our safety.

5

Q: Are you promoting lockdown drills? Because I am completely against that.

A: Lockdown drills are not the purpose of this project. Rather we want to provide general skills in order to survive active shooter training.

6

Q: What type of training are you proposing?

A: The University of Oregon can provide engaging materials that are designed to inform and educate students about what to do in an active shooter situation.
